The Sound of Goodbye

i saw him tonight,
a stranger on the street.
his pace was quick,
hands buried in his pockets,
eyes set on somewhere else.

we didn’t speak,
but somehow,
i still heard
his voice inside my chest —
soft,
familiar,
and once, only mine.

i kept my gaze low,
heart caught between
what was
and what is.

we passed —
shoulder to shoulder,
yet worlds apart.
silence heavy enough,
it drowned out
all the goodbyes
we never said.

funny, isn’t it?
how that stranger
once were my everything?
how easily
people can go
from people you know
to people you don’t.

but i wonder —
does that stranger
still remember
the sound
of my goodbye?

.
.
.
